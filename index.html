Welcome to Reserva

What is Reserva?

Reserva is a virtual currency with two main objectives:

  • Lower transaction costs for day to day transactions.
  • Increase in value over time.

Is Reserva a cryptocurrency?

No. Cryptocurrencies are decentralized. Reserva has a central authority, the Reserva Central Bank.

Fiat currencies

Fiat currencies are the world's dominant currencies - the Dollar, the Euro, the Yen, etc. Most fiat currency central banks adjust their money supply to target an inflation rate of 2-3% a year, with occasional higher spikes. In contrast, Reserva's central bank adjusts the money supply to achieve appreciation.

Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies, while having some value as a speculative asset, aren't very good for day to day purchases. The best known cryptocurrency network, Bitcoin, can only process around 5 transactions per second in the present day. Obviously, that isn't fast enough.

There are lots of really smart people trying to solve this problem. However, until someone solves it, a centralized transaction processing system is the only way to keep up with the amount of transactions created by the modern economy.

Why do merchants accept Reserva?

Reserva charges very low transaction fees, and merchants can immediately (and automatically) exchange Reservas for dollars at a guaranteed rate.
